Pasta and Onions

Homemade Pasta with Saute√©d Onions and Garlic

Pasta and Onions, a delicious family recipe passed on from generations. The aroma of these two allium vegetables sizzling in olive oil will fill the air of your kitchen, leaving a sweet, mouth-watering smell. The flavors of this dish are incredible, and holds an immense amount of flavor to them. A simple dish, yet so easy to make.

Al dente pasta, the perfect consistency, paired with sweet, caramelized onions and garlic, topped with savory Parmesan, black pepper, and a dash of basil.

Ingredients

  • 3 Tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 lb. rotini pasta
  • 2 lb. onions (about three large onions), chopped into small cubes
  • 5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan, finely grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  1. First, Heat olive oil in a large pan. Add onions and garlic and cook, stirring occasionally, until very tender and deeply golden brown, about 30 minutes. (Stop before the onions become dry and shriveled.) Add 1 1/2 tsp. salt. Remove pan from heat.
  2. Meanwhile, cook pasta in a large pot of boiling salted water, stirring occasionally, until al dente.
  3. Combine pasta and onion mixture in a large bowl. Add Parmesan, basil, and a few pinchfuls of pepper.
  4. Finally, dish out the pasta into bowls, topping with more parmesan and basil (as preffered).

Elote (Mexican Street Corn)

A Simple, Sweet, and Savory Treat

Elote (pronounced eh-loh-tay) is Mexican street corn. A delicious, quick and easy meal that will leave you and whoever is eating wanting more.

In Mexico, elote means an ear of corn, and this recipe replicates a popular serving style sold by street vendors. I can't claim that this elote recipe is quite authentic, but it brings me back to the elotes I enjoyed in Mexico.

Elote is made with corn on the cob, slathered with mayonnaise, seasoned with chili powder, and sprinkled all over with salty Cotija cheese. It's messy to eat but worth every bite. Elote is the perfect summertime side dish for any Mexican-inspired meal

Ingredients

  • 4 cobs of corn
  • 1/2 cup cotija cheese
  • 1/2 tbsp. chile powder
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 4 pairs of corn holders/skewers
  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Shuck the corn, remove the silk and stalk. Add corn to water, cover and cook for 5 minutes, then remove.
  2. When corn is cool enough to touch but still warm, insert corn holders into both ends.
  3. Brush corn with 1 tablespoon of mayonnaise, sprinkle with 2 tablespoons cheese. Add chili powder to taste.
  4. Repeat with remaining ears of corn and serve.

Juicy Oven Baked Chicken Breast

A Delicious Way to Eat Chicken

This perfect and juicy baked chicken will be sure to get a WOW at your next family get-together! It is seasoned with a delightful array of spices and baked to perfection! Your tastebuds will thank you later!

This chicken is amazing and you will not believe how easy it is to make. Within 5 minutes, you will have this chicken seasoned and ready to bake. It doesn't get much better than that!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. chicken breasts
  • 1 tbsp. brown sugar
  • 1 tsp. chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp. paprika
  • 1 tsp. Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp. garlic powder
  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees. Prepare your chicken by using a rubber mallet to pound the chicken to even thickness.
  2. In a small bowl add the brown sugar, chili powder, paprika, Italian seasoning, and garlic powder.
  3. Rub evenly and on both sides of the chicken.
  4. Grease a 9x13 inch pan and add the chicken. Bake in preheated oven for 15-20 minutes or until a thermometer reads 165 degrees.
  5. Remove the chicken from the oven and let rest for 10 minutes to adsorb the juices.
